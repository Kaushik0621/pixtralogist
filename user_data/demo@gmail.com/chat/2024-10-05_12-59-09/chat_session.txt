Here are some common findings that might be seen on a chest X-ray:

1. **Pneumonia**: This shows as areas of increased opacity (whiteness) in the lung fields, which could indicate infection or inflammation.
2. **Pleural Effusion**: This appears as a dark (black) area between the lung and the chest wall, indicating fluid accumulation in the pleural space.
3. **Pneumothorax**: This shows as a dark area on the chest X-ray with a sharp edge, indicating air in the pleural space, often caused by a collapsed lung.
4. **Heart Enlargement**: The heart appears larger than normal, which can indicate conditions like heart failure or other cardiac issues.
5. **Broken Ribs**: These can be seen as fractures in the rib cage.
6. **Lung Nodules**: These appear as small, round, white spots in the lung fields, which could indicate various conditions, including infections, cancer, or other issues.
7. **Atelectasis**: This shows as areas of reduced lung volume, often appearing as a collapse or consolidation of part of the lung.
