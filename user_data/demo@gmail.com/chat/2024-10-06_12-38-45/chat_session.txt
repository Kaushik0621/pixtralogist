Here are some common parameters found in blood reports and their general meanings:

1. **Complete Blood Count (CBC)**
   - White Blood Cells (WBC) / Leukocytes: Helps diagnose infections, allergies, and leukemia.
   - Red Blood Cells (RBC) / Erythrocytes: Helps diagnose anemia and other blood disorders.
   - Hemoglobin (Hb) & Hematocrit (Hct): Oxygen-carrying proteins and the proportion of red blood cells in the blood, respectively.
   - Platelets: Helps in blood clotting.

2. **Blood Chemistry**
   - Glucose: Sugar level in the blood, important for diagnosing diabetes.
   - Cholesterol (Total, LDL, HDL): Important for heart health and diagnosing lipid disorders.
   - Triglycerides: Another type of fat in the blood, important for heart health.
   - Electrolytes (Sodium, Potassium, Chloride): Essential minerals for various bodily functions.

3. **Liver Function Tests (LFTs)**
   - Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT) & Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST): Enzymes that help diagnose liver diseases.
   - Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP): An enzyme that can be elevated in liver or bone diseases.
   - Bilirubin: A waste product that can indicate liver or gallbladder problems.

4. **Kidney Function Tests**
   - Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN) & Creatinine: Waste products filtered by the kidneys, important for diagnosing kidney diseases.
