For a cough, you might consider the following:

1. **Decongestants**: Pseudoephedrine (Sudafed) or phenylephrine (Sudafed PE) can help relieve coughs caused by postnasal drip or sinus congestion. They are available in both tablet and liquid forms.

2. **Expectorants**: Guaifenesin (Mucinex) helps loosen and thin mucus, making it easier to cough up. This can be especially helpful with wet or productive coughs.

3. **Cough suppressants**: Dextromethorphan (Robitussin DM, Delsym) helps suppress the cough reflex, providing relief from dry, hacking coughs.

4. **Combination medicines**: Many OTC products combine decongestants, expectorants, and cough suppressants. Examples include Robitussin Cold & Cough, Mucinex D, and Triaminic All-in-One Cold.

Here are a few tips:
- Always check the label for appropriate dosing based on your age and health condition.
- Don't use multiple products containing the same active ingredient to avoid an overdose.
- If you're taking other medications, check with your pharmacist to ensure there are no drug interactions.
- If your cough is accompanied by fever, severe headache, rash, or shortness of breath, or if it persists for more than a week or two, see your doctor immediately.

Stay hydrated, rest, and keep warm. You can also try gargling with salt water or using a humidifier to soothe your throat.
